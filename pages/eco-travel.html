Nature & Landscapes — The Great Outdoors

Start your 2026 resolutions with nature. From the rugged Rockies to the sandy Florida beaches, explore America's diverse natural beauty.

Yellowstone National Park

The world's first national park, famous for its geothermal features and wildlife.

  • Visit: Old Faithful Geyser and Grand Prismatic Spring.
  • Activity: Wildlife watching in Lamar Valley.
  • Tip: Book campsites months in advance.

The Rocky Mountains

Stretching across North America, the Rockies offer dramatic peaks and alpine lakes.

  • Highlights: Rocky Mountain National Park in Colorado.
  • Winter: World-class skiing in Aspen and Vail.

The Grand Canyon

A mile-deep gorge carved by the Colorado River, revealing millions of years of geological history.

  • View: Mather Point on the South Rim.
  • Hike: The Bright Angel Trail.

Pacific Coast Highway

One of the most scenic drives in the world, hugging the California coastline.

  • Stop: Big Sur and Bixby Creek Bridge.
  • Relax: Santa Monica Pier.

Yosemite National Park

Famed for its giant ancient sequoia trees and the granite cliffs of El Capitan and Half Dome.

  • Location: California's Sierra Nevada mountains.
  • Hiking: The Mist Trail to Vernal Fall.

Great Smoky Mountains

America's most visited national park, known for its mist-covered mountains and biodiversity.

  • Hike: Clingmans Dome for 360-degree views.
  • History: Cades Cove historic buildings.

The Great Lakes

The largest group of freshwater lakes on Earth, offering beaches, dunes, and islands.

  • Visit: Sleeping Bear Dunes in Michigan.
  • Activity: Kayaking around the Apostle Islands.

FAQs

Where is Yellowstone?

Yellowstone National Park is located primarily in Wyoming, extending into Montana and Idaho.

Can I hike in the Grand Canyon?

Yes, but hiking to the bottom and back in one day is not recommended due to heat and elevation.

When is the best time for nature trips?

Spring and Fall are ideal to avoid summer crowds and extreme temperatures.
