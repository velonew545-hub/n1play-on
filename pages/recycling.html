Recycling & Waste — Simple Guide

Start the year right: Follow local rules and keep streams clean to improve outcomes.

Sorting Basics

Recycling rules vary by municipality in the US. Most areas use a single-stream or dual-stream system.

  • Blue Bin: Typically for paper, cardboard, metal cans, and plastic bottles.
  • Glass: Often collected separately or in the main bin depending on the city.
  • Check Local Rules: Always verify what plastics (e.g., #1, #2, #5) are accepted in your area.

Bottle Bills (Deposit Laws)

Several states (like CA, MI, NY) have "Bottle Bills" where you pay a deposit on beverage containers and get it back upon return.

  • Redeem: Return cans and bottles to reverse vending machines or redemption centers.
  • Value: Typically 5¢ or 10¢ per container.

Organics & Compost

Food scraps can become compost where supported.

  • Use community programs where available
  • Avoid contamination

Hard-to-Recycle Items

Some items need special drop-off points.

  • Store safely until drop-off
  • Follow label instructions

Reduce & Reuse

Recycling is one step; reduction and reuse often have greater impact.

  • Buy durable goods
  • Repair when possible

FAQs

Why rinse containers?

To reduce contamination and odours in recycling bins.

Can soft plastics be recycled?

Check local programs; availability varies.

What about e-waste?

Use designated collection points for electronics.
