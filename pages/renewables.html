Culture & History — Timeless America

Step back in time and explore the rich heritage, art, and architecture of America this New Year.

Statue of Liberty

A universal symbol of freedom and democracy, standing in New York Harbor.

  • Access: Take a ferry from Battery Park.
  • Tip: Reserve crown tickets months in advance.

The Smithsonian Institution

The world's largest museum, education, and research complex, located in Washington D.C.

  • Air & Space: See the Wright Flyer and Apollo 11 module.
  • Natural History: Home to the Hope Diamond.

Route 66

The "Main Street of America," stretching from Chicago to Santa Monica.

  • Highlights: Cadillac Ranch in Texas and the Santa Monica Pier.
  • Vibe: Classic diners and neon signs.

Independence Hall

The birthplace of the United States in Philadelphia, where the Declaration of Independence was signed.

  • Liberty Bell: Located just across the street.
  • History: Take a guided tour to see the Assembly Room.

UNESCO World Heritage

The U.S. boasts numerous UNESCO sites, ranging from cultural landmarks to natural wonders.

  • Mesa Verde: Ancient Pueblo cliff dwellings in Colorado.
  • Monticello: The plantation home of Thomas Jefferson in Virginia.

FAQs

Do I need to book tickets in advance?

Yes, for popular sites like the Statue of Liberty or Alcatraz, booking weeks in advance is recommended.

Are Smithsonian museums free?

Yes, admission to all Smithsonian museums in Washington D.C. is free.

What is Route 66?

It is a historic highway running from Chicago to Santa Monica, famous for Americana culture.
